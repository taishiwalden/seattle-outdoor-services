How to get rid of Unwanted Grass from Your Flower Beds: Effective Methods for Removal

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Seattle's Home & Garden Blog

Posted on February 26, 2023

It is common to encounter the need to remove grass that grows in your flower beds. Grass has a tendency to spread effortlessly through stray seeds and its root system.

The simplest approach is prevention, which involves maintaining your lawn properly. Avoid allowing the grass to produce seeds unless you intentionally want to preserve an area for wildlife. Additionally, promptly address any stray grass as soon as you notice its appearance.

However, if grass seeds begin to sprout or emerge from the soil in your flower beds, here are the steps to follow.

How To Get Rid Of Grass Growing In Your Flower Beds

1. Weed Out Grass By Hand

hen it comes to eliminating grass from flower beds, the most effective approach is to manually remove it, similar to dealing with other types of weeds. If the grass has spread over a large area, it is advisable to tackle small sections at a time to avoid feeling overwhelmed. To ensure effectiveness, it's crucial to remove the entire root of the grass, not just the top section of the plant.

In cases where large sections of grass need to be removed from a flower bed, an electric tiller can be helpful in quickly accomplishing the job, although some cleanup may be required afterward.

2. Install A Lawn Edging

One effective solution is to create lawn edging, which makes the task of separating the grass easier. While there are various affordable options available, we suggestsconsidering Corten steel for a sleek and contemporary appearance. The Home Depot offers a good selection of Corten steel edging, known for its attractive warm tones and durability, which adds a sophisticated finishing touch to any landscaping project.

We also suggest using materials such as bricks, rocks, tiles, or metal or plastic edging that can be installed by sinking them three or four inches into the ground. This method helps effectively stop grass from encroaching into flower beds, maintaining the desired separation.

3. Mulch Flower Beds

Another option to deal with grass in flower beds is to suppress it by applying a thick layer of mulch. This method offers the additional advantage of enhancing the soil health in your flower beds. Mulch can take various forms, including homemade compost, leaf mold, gravel, and more, each with its own benefits.

FBy applying a generous layer of mulch, you can effectively smother the grass before it establishes a foothold, ultimately reducing the amount of maintenance required in the future.

4. Use A Natural Pesticide

You can opt for organic methods rather than chemical herbicides when it comes to eliminating grass in flower beds. Chemical herbicides can be indiscriminate, killing not only the grass but also other plants it comes into contact with, including ornamental grasses in your borders.

One option to consider is using a flame gun, which utilizes intense heat to swiftly kill the grass. However, caution must be exercised to avoid accidentally harming the ornamental plants in your flower beds. The Flame King's Propane Torch Weed Burner, available on Amazon, is recommended as a reliable purchase.

Another method involves pouring boiling water over the weeds, but this primarily works on young, newly-emerging grass. The boiling water kills the foliage, while established roots remain unaffected. It's important to note that the grass may regrow quickly, and care must be taken when applying boiling water in flower beds to avoid damaging other plants.

DIY sprays can also be created, such as a mixture of vinegar, soap, and salt, which can be applied to newly-emerged grass to kill it. However, it's worth mentioning that regular use of this mixture can lead to a buildup of salt in the soil, which could potentially harm your ornamental plants.
